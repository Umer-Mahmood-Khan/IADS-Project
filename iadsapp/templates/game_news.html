Every Major Video Game Release Coming Soon For Xbox Series X|S And Xbox One
Every upcoming new game release for the Xbox Series X|S and Xbox one
BY RUSS BOSWELL AND MARK SAMMUT

As the Xbox Series X/S enjoys its latest year, Microsoft's platform is gradually amassing a respectable library. Consoles typically do not start to flourish until the latter half of their existence, once cross-gen stops being a priority and developers can focus exclusively on crafting projects for only the most modern systems. The Xbox Series X/S has yet to reach that point, although things are moving in that direction.

The last few years have produced plenty of fantastic games, particularly in the open-world genre. Elden Ring, Lego Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga, A Plague Tale: Requiem, and Tiny Tina's Wonderlands were all good or great 2022 releases, and they offered wildly different experiences from each other. The year's successor largely followed suit with projects like Dead Space, Street Fighter 6, Hi-Fi Rush, Remnant 2, Forza Motorsport, and Lies of P. What Xbox games are coming soon? Which titles are expected to drop in 2024?

February 2024 Xbox Series X/S & Xbox One Games
Suicide Squad, Skull And Bones, & More

2024 got into the swing of things in the second half of January, paving the way to a fairly packed February. Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League, Skull And Bones, and Tomb Raider I-III Remastered Starring Lara Croft are just a few standout titles, and while they have not all led smooth lives, they are nevertheless major names. Persona 3 Reload might prove to be February 2024's best Xbox game, and it launched directly on Game Pass. While Persona 3 is quite accessible in this day and age due to its many, many ports, the original game and its various versions show their ages in a few ways. Just to be clear, they are still worth playing; however, a modern remake should make this story more accessible to new players.

A new Rocksteady release should be a cause for celebration, but Suicide Squad: Kill the Justice League has debuted to a very mixed response. While not completely without value, the game is the definition of polarizing. The same might end up being the case for Skull and Bones, and there seems to be little excitement around Ubisoft's pirate-themed game. Don't Nod's Banishers: Ghosts of New Eden has garnered a generally positive reception, and it might hit the mark with fans of the developer's previous games such as Vampyr.
